How Type 2 Diabetes Affects Health

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) affects multiple systems in the body and often develops silently over several years.

  • Blood Sugar Regulation: Persistent high glucose damages blood vessels and nerves.
  • Cardiovascular Health: Increased risk of heart attack, stroke, and hypertension.
  • Kidney Function: Long-term strain can lead to chronic kidney disease.
  • Nerve Damage: Peripheral neuropathy causes pain, numbness, and foot complications.
  • Vision: High glucose can damage retinal blood vessels, leading to diabetic retinopathy.

Based on information from: NHS & World Health Organization (WHO)

Evidence-Based Management Tips

Small, consistent lifestyle changes can significantly reduce the risk of developing or worsening Type 2 diabetes.

  • Regular Activity: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week.
  • Balanced Nutrition: Prioritise whole grains, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats.
  • Weight Management: Even a 5–7% weight reduction can lower risk.
  • Sleep & Stress: Good sleep and stress management support metabolic health.
  • Routine Check-ups: Regular blood tests help detect changes early.
