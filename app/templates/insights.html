Insights From Data

These insights summarise what the machine learning models learned from the dataset, highlighting the strongest predictors of Type 2 diabetes, fairness considerations, and clinically meaningful patterns.

1. Key Predictors Identified by the Model

The XGBoost model consistently identified the following features as the strongest predictors of Type 2 diabetes:

  • Age : risk increases steadily with age, especially after 45.
  • BMI : one of the most influential predictors; higher BMI strongly correlates with risk.
  • Systolic Blood Pressure : elevated SBP contributes significantly to predicted risk.
  • Ethnicity : certain ethnic groups show higher baseline risk, consistent with NHS data.
  • Sex : small but measurable differences in risk distribution.

These findings align with established clinical evidence and support the model’s validity.

2. Patterns Observed in the Dataset

  • Higher BMI groups showed disproportionately higher diabetes prevalence.
  • Individuals with high SBP and DBP had significantly elevated predicted risk.
  • Age and BMI combined created the strongest risk clusters.
  • Ethnic disparities were present, requiring fairness evaluation.

These patterns informed both the clinical and lifestyle risk pathways.

3. Fairness & Bias Insights

Fairness analysis revealed differences in recall across demographic groups. The fairness dashboard highlights:

  • Recall gaps between ethnic groups.
  • False negative disparities in certain subgroups.
  • Threshold sensitivity small threshold changes affect fairness.

These insights motivated the Performance vs Fairness Comparison section, helping identify thresholds that balance accuracy and fairness.

4. Model Performance Summary

  • XGBoost achieved the highest AUC and best overall performance.
  • Random Forest performed well but less consistently across subgroups.
  • Logistic Regression provided interpretability and a strong baseline.

These results justify the use of XGBoost in the deployed clinical and non‑clinical pathways.

5. What This Means for Users

The insights from the dataset help ensure that predictions are:

  • Clinically meaningful — aligned with NHS‑recognised risk factors.
  • Fair — evaluated across demographic groups.
  • Transparent — supported by explainability and threshold analysis.

These insights directly inform the design of the risk pathways and the fairness dashboard.
