Help & Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about how this tool works and what your results mean.

About the Tool

Is this a medical diagnosis?

No. This tool provides a statistical estimate of diabetes risk based on general clinical indicators. It is not a diagnosis.

How does the tool calculate risk?

The system uses a machine learning model trained on real health data, combined with simple clinical rules based on widely recognised health guidelines.

Why does the tool ask for blood pressure and BMI?

These are common indicators associated with diabetes risk and help the model estimate your likelihood more accurately.

Understanding Your Results

What does “Low”, “Medium”, or “High” risk mean?

These categories reflect the model’s estimate of your likelihood of having or developing diabetes. They are not diagnostic.

What should I do if my risk is high?

If you are concerned about your results, consider speaking to a healthcare professional for personalised advice.

Why do I see “Clinical Rules Triggered”?

Some health indicators, such as very high blood pressure or BMI, are known risk factors. When these appear, the system highlights them for transparency.

Data & Privacy

Is my data stored?

This prototype does not store personal data. It processes your inputs temporarily to generate a risk estimate.

How is my information used?

Your inputs are used only to calculate your risk score. No personal information is saved or shared.
